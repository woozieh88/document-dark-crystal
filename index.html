1. Gambaran Umum

Premis Inti

Manhunt adalah PvP map asimetris untuk 6-16 siswa usia 8-15 tahun. Tim kecil Runners mendapat head start ke dunia jungle yang padat. Hunters adalah semua pemain yang tersisa dan dilepas sesaat kemudian dengan Tracking Compass yang diperbarui setiap 20 detik, mengarah ke Runner terdekat. Runners menang dengan bertahan hidup sampai timer ronde habis. Hunters menang dengan mengeliminasi semua Runners sebelum waktu habis.

Implementasi Builder

Dunia harus terbaca sebagai arena kejar-kejaran terlebih dahulu dan pemandangan jungle setelahnya. Setiap landmark, percabangan rute, mulut cave, jembatan canopy, dan penyeberangan air harus membantu pemain memahami di mana tekanan meningkat, di mana ruang sembunyi tersedia, dan di mana Hunters dapat berkumpul kembali setelah setiap update Tracking Compass.

Implementasi Developer

Loop ronde membutuhkan state controller yang deterministik dengan minimal state hub, ready check, pelepasan Runner, head start, pelepasan Hunter, combat phase, hasil ronde, role swap, dan session end. Role pemain harus dilacak secara eksplisit melalui tag atau scoreboard agar win logic, loadout, spectator mode, dan telemetry semuanya membaca dari state yang sama.

Gameplay / Batasan

Premis ini hanya berfungsi jika Runners dapat berpindah posisi secara berarti di antara update compass dan jika Hunters harus berkomunikasi alih-alih menerima live tracking. Skala arena, pemutus sightline, cadence update, dan timing head start karena itu harus tetap selaras dengan kurva tensi kelas yang disebutkan di brief.

Rotasi Role

Role berganti setiap ronde agar semua siswa merasakan kedua sisi permainan. Map ini dirancang di sekitar tensi update compass: Hunters harus berkomunikasi dan berkumpul ulang setiap 20 detik, sedangkan Runners harus terus bergerak, memakai terrain dengan cerdas, dan memasang trap untuk bertahan hidup.

Implementasi Builder

Rotasi role berarti map tidak boleh mengistimewakan satu sisi dengan pengetahuan tersembunyi yang hanya terlihat melalui permainan berulang. Penanda traversal, jalur trap, pemutus visibilitas, dan keterbacaan landmark harus cukup adil agar siswa bisa berganti role setelah satu ronde tanpa perlu penjelasan desainer.

Implementasi Developer

Role swap harus sepenuhnya otomatis. Cleanup di akhir ronde harus menghapus efek sementara, mengosongkan inventory, mereset timer, memulihkan logic arena, dan membalik assignment Runner dan Hunter tanpa bergantung pada cleanup manual operator di antara ronde.

Gameplay / Batasan

Ritme 20 detik adalah mekanik inti pembelajaran dan tensi, bukan timer kosmetik. Jalan pintas implementasi apa pun yang secara efektif mengubah Tracking Compass menjadi live tracking atau membuat Hunters tidak memiliki beat update yang jelas akan mengubah pengalaman yang dimaksud.

Arah Environment

Setting jungle dipilih dengan sengaja - canopy yang padat, caves, rivers, dan ruins memberi Runners opsi bertahan hidup yang benar-benar kreatif sambil membuat Hunters tetap aktif dan terus berkoordinasi.

Implementasi Builder

Environment membutuhkan traversal berlapis, bukan sekadar dekorasi padat. Canopy, jungle floor, cabang cave, chokepoint river, dan ruins harus membentuk jaringan kejar-kejaran yang saling terhubung, di mana setiap zone menawarkan tipe pelarian atau titik runtuh yang berbeda.

Implementasi Developer

Mekanik environment harus memperkuat identitas zone yang dijelaskan di brief: penyeberangan river sebagai area terbuka, caves sebagai ruang pembagi tekanan, canopy sebagai mobilitas vertikal yang berisiko, dan ruins sebagai cover yang padat. Detection, trap placement, dan landing buff semuanya harus terikat pada peran zone ini, bukan ditambahkan secara acak.

Gameplay / Batasan

Jungle harus terasa penuh petualangan dan premium, bukan menakutkan atau berisik secara visual. Lighting, kepadatan foliage, dan kontras material perlu membantu pemain yang lebih muda membaca pergerakan dan bahaya dengan cepat selama permainan berlangsung.

2. Spesifikasi Inti

Konfigurasi Sesi

Pemain: 6 sampai 16. Runners: 3 pemain untuk sesi 10-16 pemain dan 2 pemain untuk sesi di bawah 10. Hunters: semua pemain yang tersisa. Durasi ronde: 8-10 menit per ronde. Dua ronde per sesi dengan role swap di antara ronde.

Implementasi Builder

Kapasitas hub, gerbang pelepasan, lebar cave, penyeberangan river, dan ruang pertemuan utama harus diuji pada kedua ujung rentang jumlah pemain. Arena tidak boleh terasa kosong pada 6 pemain atau terlalu bertabrakan pada 16 pemain.

Implementasi Developer

Selector, role assignment, ready check, win logic, dan hitungan scoreboard harus dinamis dan diturunkan dari pemain aktif saat runtime. Tidak ada command chain yang boleh bergantung pada nama tetap atau jumlah pemain yang di-hardcode.

Gameplay / Batasan

Arena harus menopang pergerakan hide-and-seek yang bermakna untuk sesi kelas yang singkat. Jika ronde memakai durasi 8-10 menit, Runners membutuhkan ruang relokasi yang cukup agar Hunters bergantung pada ritme Tracking Compass, bukan tekanan spawn langsung.

Hasil Match dan Timing

Match Format: Runners bertahan sampai timer habis atau Hunters mengeliminasi semua Runners. Win Condition: Runners: keduanya hidup saat timer berakhir. Hunters: semua Runners tereliminasi sebelum timer. Compass Update: compass Hunters diperbarui setiap 20 detik mengarah ke Runner terdekat. Bukan live tracking.

Implementasi Builder

Map harus mendukung kemenangan berbasis survival dan kemenangan berbasis eliminasi secara setara. Itu membutuhkan rute loop-back yang kuat untuk Runners, tetapi tidak boleh ada tempat sembunyi yang tidak terjangkau dan memungkinkan stalling pasif tanpa batas.

Implementasi Developer

Logic hasil ronde harus membaca langsung dari jumlah Runner yang masih hidup, round timer, dan role tag. Loop compass harus berjalan pada cadence 20 detik yang presisi, menyiarkan feedback yang jelas, dan selalu menyelesaikan target Runner terdekat yang valid.

Gameplay / Batasan

Spesifikasi ini mendefinisikan map sebagai game kejar-kejaran dengan gap informasi. Hunters membutuhkan cukup informasi agar tetap aktif, tetapi tidak sampai menghapus concealment. Runners membutuhkan cukup waktu di antara update untuk berpindah, tetapi tidak sampai menghilang ke loop aman yang dominan.

Alternative Note: Aturan jumlah Runner mengizinkan 3 Runners pada sesi 10-16 pemain, tetapi win condition mengatakan "keduanya hidup saat timer berakhir." Kunci aturan menjadi "semua Runners yang masih hidup harus bertahan" atau maksimum tetap 2 Runners sebelum implementasi.

Ready dan Spawn Systems

Ready System: Tick-ready system di Spawn Hub. Setiap pemain mengonfirmasi ready melalui klik NPC. Ready wall menampilkan semua pemain dengan status ready atau waiting. Ronde tidak dimulai sampai semua pemain ready atau teacher melakukan override. Spawn System: Shared Spawn Hub dengan leaderboard, ready wall, rules board, dan role assignment NPC. Map Theme: Dunia jungle yang padat - ancient ruins, river systems, cave networks, jungle canopy, waterfalls.

Implementasi Builder

Spawn Hub harus mendukung pengumpulan pra-ronde, pembacaan aturan, visibilitas status, dan pengawasan teacher tanpa menghalangi pergerakan. Pembacaan visual pertama harus langsung menampilkan ready wall, leaderboard wall, titik role assignment, dan arsitektur pelepasan.

Implementasi Developer

Loop ready harus menjadi pemilik otoritas start sesi. Ronde tidak boleh dimulai sampai semua pemain aktif memiliki ready tag atau teacher override secara eksplisit melewati pengecekan tersebut. Tampilan ready wall apa pun harus membaca dari state yang sama dengan logic gate dan penguncian role.

Gameplay / Batasan

Hub adalah bagian dari pacing kelas, bukan pengisi. Siswa membutuhkan tempat yang jelas untuk mereset pemahaman di antara ronde, sementara tema jungle tetap harus terasa sejak pembacaan kamera pertama.

3. Role & Loadout

Role Assignment

Roles diassign di Spawn Hub sebelum setiap ronde. Teacher atau role assignment NPC menentukan Runners dan Hunters. Semua gear diterapkan otomatis melalui command block pada saat role assignment.

Implementasi Builder

Titik role assignment harus terlihat, mudah dibaca, dan terpisah secara fisik dari leaderboard serta ready wall agar pemain memahami kapan mereka sedang diassign dan kapan mereka hanya sedang berkumpul.

Implementasi Developer

Role assignment harus menjadi satu titik kepemilikan untuk tag, inventory, armor, efek, dan reset skor. Logic assignment tidak boleh diduplikasi di NPC, gate, dan start timer karena itu akan membuat loadout dan telemetry mudah drift.

Gameplay / Batasan

Siswa harus langsung memahami sisi mereka. Konfirmasi visual dan audio harus membuat jelas siapa Runner dan siapa Hunter sebelum countdown pelepasan dimulai.

Alternative Note: Section 3 menyebut teacher atau role assignment NPC yang menentukan role, sedangkan Section 5.1 menyebut role diassign secara acak saat ronde dimulai. Satu otoritas assignment harus dipilih sebelum scripting dan QA.

Runners

Runners menerima Food (10x Cooked Chicken), Iron Axe, 2x Speed Potion (Speed I, masing-masing 30 detik), 3x tripwire trap kit yang sudah disiapkan, dan Leather armour. Runners mendapat head start 30 detik saat Hunters dikunci di holding cage. Countdown 30 detik terlihat oleh kedua tim. Runners tidak bisa sprint selama 5 detik pertama setelah gerbang dibuka.

Implementasi Builder

Titik spawn Runner membutuhkan akses langsung ke minimal dua rute yang bermakna dan satu keputusan concealment dalam beberapa detik pertama. Tidak ada spawn Runner yang boleh langsung mengarah ke jalan buntu atau ke visibilitas penuh dari garis pelepasan Hunter.

Implementasi Developer

Paket Runner harus diterapkan secara atomik saat assignment dan divalidasi ulang saat release. Logic head start, pembatasan sprint, inventory trap, serta title atau sound feedback harus membaca dari state Runner yang sama agar alur awal ronde tidak desync.

Gameplay / Batasan

Runners dirancang untuk bertahan hidup dan berimprovisasi, bukan untuk duel. Set item mereka harus mendukung movement, pelarian sementara, dan pemecahan masalah berbasis environment sambil menjaga direct combat tetap lebih lemah daripada tekanan Hunter.

Alternative Note: Section 7 menyebut Runners langsung sprint saat release, tetapi section ini menyebut Runners tidak bisa sprint selama 5 detik pertama. Pertahankan satu aturan movement awal.

Hunters

Hunters menerima Tracking Compass, Iron Sword, Bow plus 16 Arrows, Iron Boots dengan Feather Falling, dan Food (10x Cooked Chicken). Hunters tidak bisa sprint selama 60 detik pertama setelah release. Compass diperbarui setiap 20 detik dan tidak memberikan live tracking.

Implementasi Builder

Ruang pelepasan Hunter, jalur jungle pertama yang terlihat, dan titik regroup pertama harus mendukung pursuit yang tertunda tetapi terkoordinasi. Hunters membutuhkan cukup informasi sightline untuk memilih arah setelah release tanpa langsung melihat semua rute Runner.

Implementasi Developer

Paket Hunter harus diterapkan bersama efek sprint-delay dan state aktivasi compass. Tracking Compass tidak boleh aktif sebelum momen pelepasan Hunter atau saat fase holding cage masih berlangsung.

Gameplay / Batasan

Sprint delay adalah tuas balancing yang membuat head start benar-benar bernilai. Efek ini harus cukup membatasi untuk melindungi Runners di awal, tetapi tidak sampai membuat Hunters kehilangan menit pertama karena kebingungan alih-alih pursuit yang terorganisir.

6. Tracking Compass

Mengapa 20 Detik

20 detik adalah interval optimal untuk sesi kelas karena menjaga Hunters tetap bergerak, memberi waktu kepada Runners untuk berpindah, menciptakan beat tensi yang berulang, dan mencegah map berubah menjadi pure chase yang didominasi Hunters yang lebih cepat.

Implementasi Builder

Panjang rute, kedalaman cabang cave, loop canopy, dan exposure river semuanya perlu dituning agar update 20 detik terasa bermakna. Jika arena terlalu kecil, interval ini akan terasa hampir seperti live tracking; jika arena terlalu besar, Hunters akan menghabiskan update hanya untuk mendapatkan orientasi dasar.

Implementasi Developer

Cadence compass harus dijadwalkan secara presisi dan tidak boleh drift karena command chain yang saling tumpang tindih. Audio, actionbar feedback, dan update direction harus menjadi satu transaksi update agar pemain merasakan satu beat yang jelas, bukan beberapa event yang terpisah.

Gameplay / Batasan

Interval update adalah aturan balancing, bukan preferensi kosmetik. Menguranginya akan mengubah map dari survival asimetris menjadi pursuit langsung. Menaikkannya terlalu jauh akan membuat Hunters bergantung pada keberuntungan alih-alih teamwork.

Urutan Update Compass

Setiap update memutar sound cue untuk Hunters, menyalakan actionbar COMPASS UPDATED, memperbarui jarum compass ke arah Runner terdekat, dan juga memberikan sound cue yang berbeda kepada Runners agar mereka tahu Hunters baru saja menerima arah baru.

Implementasi Builder

Karena kedua tim mendengar beat update, arena harus mendukung reaksi langsung. Pilihan rute terdekat, titik jatuh, dan transisi cover perlu tersedia setiap kali Runner mendengar cue itu, atau feedback akan terasa informatif tetapi tidak bisa ditindaklanjuti.

Implementasi Developer

Sistem membutuhkan query Runner-terdekat yang andal, metode penargetan compass yang konsisten, dan aset audio yang terpisah agar Hunters serta Runners sama-sama memahami event itu dari perspektif role mereka. Solusi fallback apa pun harus tetap menjaga ritme 20 detik dan feedback tiap role meskipun implementasi arah compass berubah.

Gameplay / Batasan

Audio cue yang didengar bersama sengaja memberi tekanan pada Runners untuk berpindah posisi. Jika cue menyala tanpa update direction yang bermakna bagi Hunters, atau jika Runners tidak realistis untuk bereaksi setelah mendengarnya, mind-game utamanya akan rusak.

Alternative Note: /setworldspawn or equivalent bukan janji implementasi yang aman untuk world kelas bersama karena perubahan worldspawn dapat memengaruhi sistem di luar loop compass. Kunci metode penargetan compass yang sebenarnya sebelum implementasi command block dimulai.

10. Logika Command Block

Map akan dipakai dalam sesi singkat dengan pemain yang lebih muda. Jika state logic tulang punggung pernah macet atau hanya setengah mereset, pengalaman kelas akan rusak jauh lebih cepat dibanding lingkungan server multiplayer berdurasi panjang.

Gameplay / Batasan

Mekanik-mekanik ini menciptakan ritme emosional ronde. Jika title, sound, dan effect call bertumpuk terlalu agresif, siswa akan berhenti membacanya. Frekuensi feedback harus tetap dramatis tanpa menjadi bising.

1. Overview

Core Premise

Manhunt is an asymmetric PvP map for 6-16 students aged 8-15. A small team of Runners gets a head start into a dense jungle world. The Hunters are all remaining players and are released shortly after with a Tracking Compass that updates every 20 seconds, pointing toward the nearest Runner. Runners win by surviving the full round timer. Hunters win by eliminating all Runners before time runs out.

Builder Implementation

The world must read as a chase arena first and a scenic jungle second. Every landmark, route split, cave mouth, canopy bridge, and water crossing must help players understand where pressure rises, where concealment exists, and where Hunters can regroup after each Tracking Compass update.

Developer Implementation

The round loop needs a deterministic state controller with at least hub, ready check, Runner release, head start, Hunter release, combat phase, round result, role swap, and session end states. Player roles should be tracked explicitly through tags or scoreboards so win logic, loadouts, spectator mode, and telemetry all read from the same authoritative state.

Gameplay / Constraints

This premise only works if Runners can relocate meaningfully between compass updates and if Hunters must communicate rather than receive live tracking. Arena scale, sightline breaks, update cadence, and head start timing therefore have to stay aligned with the stated classroom tension curve.

Role Rotation

Roles swap every round so all students experience both sides. The map is designed around the tension of the compass update: Hunters must communicate and regroup every 20 seconds, and Runners must keep moving, use the terrain cleverly, and set traps to survive.

Builder Implementation

Role rotation means the map cannot privilege one side with hidden knowledge that only repeated play reveals. Traversal cues, trap lanes, visibility breaks, and landmark readability must be fair enough that students can swap roles after one round without needing designer explanation.

Developer Implementation

The role swap must be fully automated. Round-end cleanup has to remove temporary effects, clear inventories, reset timers, restore arena logic, and invert Runner and Hunter assignments without depending on manual operator cleanup between rounds.

Gameplay / Constraints

The 20-second rhythm is the core teaching and tension mechanic, not a cosmetic timer. Any implementation shortcut that effectively turns the Tracking Compass into live tracking or leaves Hunters without a clear update beat will change the intended experience.

Environment Intent

The jungle setting is intentional - dense canopy, caves, rivers, and ruins give Runners genuine options for creative survival while keeping Hunters engaged and coordinating constantly.

Builder Implementation

The environment needs layered traversal rather than decorative clutter. Canopy, jungle floor, cave branches, river chokepoints, and ruins must form a connected pursuit network where each zone offers a different type of escape or collapse point.

Developer Implementation

Environmental mechanics should reinforce the zone identity described in the brief: river crossings as exposure, caves as split-pressure spaces, canopy as risky vertical mobility, and ruins as dense cover. Detection, trap placement, and landing buffs should all be tied to these zone roles rather than added arbitrarily.

Gameplay / Constraints

The jungle must feel adventurous and premium, not frightening or visually noisy. Lighting, foliage density, and material contrast need to support younger players reading movement and danger quickly during live play.

2. Core Specifications

Session Configuration

Players: 6 to 16. Runners: 3 players for sessions of 10-16 players and 2 players for sessions under 10. Hunters: all remaining players. Round Length: 8-10 minutes per round. Two rounds per session with a role swap between rounds.

Builder Implementation

Hub capacity, release gates, cave widths, river crossings, and major encounter spaces must be tested at both ends of the player count range. The arena should not feel empty at 6 players or collision-heavy at 16 players.

Developer Implementation

Selectors, role assignment, ready checks, win logic, and scoreboard counts must be dynamic and derived from active players at runtime. No command chain should depend on fixed names or a hardcoded player count.

Gameplay / Constraints

The arena has to sustain meaningful hide-and-seek movement for a short classroom session. If 8-10 minute rounds are used, Runners need enough relocation space that Hunters rely on the Tracking Compass rhythm instead of direct spawn pressure.

Match Outcome and Timing

Match Format: Runners survive the timer or Hunters eliminate all Runners. Win Condition: Runners: both alive at timer end. Hunters: all Runners eliminated before timer. Compass Update: Hunter compasses update every 20 seconds pointing to the nearest Runner. Not live tracking.

Builder Implementation

The map must support survival-based wins and elimination-based wins equally. That requires strong loop-back routes for Runners, but no unreachable hide spots that allow indefinite passive stalling.

Developer Implementation

Round result logic should read directly from a live Runner count, a round timer, and role tags. The compass loop must execute on a precise 20-second cadence, broadcast clear feedback, and resolve the nearest eligible Runner consistently.

Gameplay / Constraints

This specification defines the map as an information-gap chase game. Hunters need enough information to stay active, but not enough to erase concealment. Runners need enough time between updates to reposition, but not enough time to disappear into a safe, dominant loop.

Alternative Note: The runner-count rule allows 3 Runners in sessions of 10-16 players, but the win condition says "both alive at timer end." Lock the rule to either "all living Runners survive" or a fixed maximum of 2 Runners before implementation.

Ready and Spawn Systems

Ready System: Tick-ready system in Spawn Hub. Each player confirms ready via NPC click. Ready wall lists all players with ready or waiting status. Round does not start until all players ready or teacher overrides. Spawn System: Shared Spawn Hub with leaderboard, ready wall, rules board, and role assignment NPC. Map Theme: Dense jungle world - ancient ruins, river systems, cave networks, jungle canopy, waterfalls.

Builder Implementation

The Spawn Hub must support pre-round gathering, rules reading, status visibility, and teacher supervision without blocking movement. The first visual read should show the ready wall, leaderboard wall, role assignment point, and release architecture clearly.

Developer Implementation

The ready loop should own session start authority. The round must not begin until either every active player has the ready tag or the teacher override explicitly bypasses the check. Any ready-wall display should read from the same state used by the gate and role-lock logic.

Gameplay / Constraints

The hub is part of the classroom pacing, not filler. Students need a clear place to reset understanding between rounds, while the jungle theme still needs to feel present from the first camera read.

3. Roles & Loadouts

Role Assignment

Roles are assigned in the Spawn Hub before each round. The teacher or a role assignment NPC designates Runners and Hunters. All gear is applied automatically via command block on role assignment.

Builder Implementation

The role assignment point must be visible, legible, and physically separated from the leaderboard and ready wall so players understand when they are being assigned versus when they are simply gathering.

Developer Implementation

Role assignment should be a single ownership point for tags, inventories, armor, effects, and score resets. Assignment logic must not be duplicated across NPCs, gates, and start timers because that creates drift in loadouts and telemetry.

Gameplay / Constraints

Students must understand their side instantly. Visual and audio confirmation should make it obvious who is a Runner and who is a Hunter before the release countdown starts.

Alternative Note: Section 3 says the teacher or a role assignment NPC designates roles, while Section 5.1 says roles are assigned randomly at round start. One assignment authority must be chosen before scripting and QA.

Runners

Runners receive Food (10x Cooked Chicken), an Iron Axe, 2x Speed Potion (Speed I, 30 seconds each), 3x pre-placed tripwire trap kits, and Leather armour. Runners have a 30-second head start while Hunters are locked in a holding cage. The 30-second countdown is visible to both teams. Runners cannot sprint for the first 5 seconds after the gate opens.

Builder Implementation

Runner spawn points need immediate access to at least two meaningful routes and one concealment decision inside the first few seconds. No Runner spawn should funnel directly into a dead-end or into full visibility from the Hunter release line.

Developer Implementation

The Runner package should be applied atomically at assignment and validated again on release. Head-start logic, sprint restriction, trap inventory, and any title or sound feedback all need to read from the same Runner state so the early-round flow cannot desync.

Gameplay / Constraints

Runners are meant to survive and improvise, not duel. Their item set should support movement, temporary escape, and environmental problem solving while keeping direct combat inferior to Hunter pressure.

Alternative Note: Section 7 says Runners sprint immediately on release, but this section says Runners cannot sprint for the first 5 seconds. Keep one start-movement rule.

Hunters

Hunters receive a Tracking Compass, an Iron Sword, a Bow plus 16 Arrows, Iron Boots with Feather Falling, and Food (10x Cooked Chicken). Hunters cannot sprint for the first 60 seconds after release. The compass updates every 20 seconds and does not provide live tracking.

Builder Implementation

Hunter release space, the first visible jungle lanes, and the first regroup point must support a delayed but coordinated pursuit. Hunters need enough sightline information to choose a direction after release without seeing every Runner route instantly.

Developer Implementation

The Hunter package should be applied together with the sprint-delay effect and the compass activation state. The Tracking Compass must not activate before the Hunter release moment or during the holding-cage phase.

Gameplay / Constraints

The sprint delay is a balancing lever that gives the head start real value. It must feel restrictive enough to protect Runners early, but not so punitive that Hunters lose the first minute to confusion instead of organized pursuit.

Gameplay / Constraints

6. The Tracking Compass

Why 20 Seconds

20 seconds is the optimal interval for a classroom session because it keeps Hunters moving, gives Runners time to relocate, creates a recurring tension beat, and prevents the map from becoming a pure chase dominated by faster Hunters.

Builder Implementation

Route lengths, cave branch depth, canopy loops, and river exposure all need to be tuned so a 20-second update is meaningful. If the arena is too small, the interval becomes almost live tracking; if the arena is too large, Hunters spend updates reacquiring basic orientation instead of making tactical choices.

Developer Implementation

The compass cadence should be scheduled precisely and not drift because of overlapping command chains. Audio, actionbar feedback, and direction updates should be part of one update transaction so players feel one clear beat rather than several disconnected events.

Gameplay / Constraints

The update interval is a balance rule, not a cosmetic preference. Reducing it changes the map from asymmetric survival to direct pursuit. Increasing it too far makes Hunters rely on luck instead of teamwork.

Compass Update Sequence

Each update plays a sound cue for Hunters, fires a COMPASS UPDATED actionbar, updates the compass needle toward the nearest Runner, and also gives Runners a distinct sound cue so they know the Hunters just received a new direction.

Builder Implementation

Because both teams hear the update beat, the arena must support immediate reaction. Nearby route choices, drop-offs, and cover transitions must be available whenever a Runner hears the cue or the feedback will feel informational but not actionable.

Developer Implementation

The system needs a reliable nearest-Runner query, a consistent compass-targeting method, and separated audio assets so Hunters and Runners can both understand the event from their own role perspective. Any fallback solution must preserve the 20-second rhythm and role feedback even if compass direction implementation changes.

Gameplay / Constraints

The shared audio cue intentionally pressures Runners to relocate. If the cue fires without meaningful direction updates for Hunters, or if Runners cannot realistically react after hearing it, the central mind-game breaks down.

Alternative Note: /setworldspawn or equivalent is not a safe implementation promise for a shared classroom world because worldspawn changes may affect systems outside the compass loop. Lock the actual compass-targeting method before command block implementation begins.

10. Command Block Logic

State Controller

Ready System, Role Assignment, Head Start Timer, Hunter Sprint Delay, Round Timer, and Auto-Reset define the backbone of the match loop.

Builder Implementation

The world must include predictable interaction points for every backbone mechanic: ready NPC location, assignment point, release gate, Hunter holding cage, reset landing zone, and any teacher override position. Physical layout and logic ownership need to match.

Developer Implementation

These systems should be built as a single state machine rather than as isolated repeating command chains. Ready state, role tags, start countdown, sprint restriction, round clock, and reset all need to share authoritative scoreboards or tags so the loop can be tested and replayed without manual cleanup.

Gameplay / Constraints

The map will be used in short sessions with younger players. If the backbone state logic ever stalls or half-resets, the classroom experience breaks faster than in a longer-form multiplayer server environment.

Live Round Systems

Tracking Compass, Runner Elimination, Round End conditions, Survival Timer, Survival Bonus Item, Trap Deployment, Hunter Spread Cue, and Waterfall Escape Buff all operate during the active round.

Builder Implementation

Live-round mechanics need physical support in the world: enough trap-friendly corridors, enough visibility for spread cues to matter, a readable waterfall landing zone, and spectator-safe boundaries that do not interfere with active play.

Developer Implementation

Each live-round system should own one event moment and should write to shared objectives only where needed. Elimination, trap triggers, survival timing, midpoint rewards, and spread cues must avoid duplicate firing through edge-trigger logic and cooldowns.

Gameplay / Constraints

These mechanics create the emotional rhythm of the round. If titles, sounds, and effect calls overlap too aggressively, students will stop reading them. Feedback frequency has to stay dramatic without becoming noisy.

Scoreboards and In-World Displays

Leaderboard Wall, Survival Timer, remaining Runner count, eliminations, session winner, and spectator entry all depend on scoreboards and world-facing feedback surfaces.

Builder Implementation

Scoreboard-facing surfaces should be placed where players naturally look during phase changes: near the hub centre, above or beside the exit flow, and in a spectator-friendly orientation after eliminations. Decorative framing should never reduce contrast or clip long player names.

Developer Implementation

Numeric objectives should remain the baseline data model. Any mirrored wall display, actionbar formatting, or spectator item behavior should be treated as a presentation layer on top of the scoreboard state, not as the state itself.

Gameplay / Constraints

Players must be able to answer three questions quickly during the session: who is still alive, who is winning the round, and what happens next. Display design and scoreboard logic should be judged against that clarity test.

Alternative Note: Live sign mirroring and spectator-compass activation are both presentation features with platform risk. Keep the scoreboard objectives and direct spectator transfer as the baseline, then layer richer presentation only after the baseline loop is stable.
